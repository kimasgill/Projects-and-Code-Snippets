Michael Jackson

The 'King of Pop'

Here's a time line of Michael Jackson's career highlights:

  • 1962 Michael becomes a member of the Jackson 5 along with his brothers Tito, Jackie, Jermaine, and Marlon as the lead singer. Their father, Joe, is their manager.
  • 1968 The Jackson 5 sign a contract with Motown records.
  • 1969 The Jackson 5 debut their first hit single “Want You Back.” The single sells over two million copies.
  • 1969 The Jackson 5 release their debut album "Diana Ross Presents The Jackson 5".
  • 1970 The Jackson 5 release the album ABC.
  • 1971 Michael releases his first solo single “Got To Be There.”
  • 1972 Michael Jackson releases his debut album Got To Be There.
  • 1976The Jackson 5 change their name to The Jacksons and get their own TV show.
  • 1978 Michael has a movie debut as a scarecrow in The Wiz, a remake of 'The Wizard of Oz'. It co-stars Diana Ross, Lena Horne, Nipsey Russell and Richard Pryor.
  • 1979 His album Off the Wall, produced by Quincy Jones, launches him to superstar standing. It sells over nineteen million copies worldwide.
  • 1980 Wins a Grammy for Best Vocal Performance with “Don’t Stop ‘Til You Get Enough.”
  • 1982 Releases 'Thriller' which sells over 50 million copies worldwide and solidifies him as the King of Pop. It remains today the best selling album of all time.
  • 1983 Performs “Billy Jean” at the Motown 25 Yesterday, Today, Forever Special and does the moonwalk for the first time. This will become his signature move.
  • 1984 Gets a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
  • 2001 He is inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

    • "I'm very honored to have been chosen. In a world filled with hate, we must still dare to hope. In a world filled with anger, we must still dare to comfort. In a world filled with despair, we must still dare to dream. And in a world filled with distrust, we must still dare to believe."

    Michael Jackson
